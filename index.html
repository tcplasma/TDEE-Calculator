📚 Understanding Your Results

🔥 What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate is the calories your body needs at complete rest. It accounts for 60-75% of daily calories.

⚡ What is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure includes BMR plus calories from daily activities and exercise.

📊 NASEM 2023 Standard

Uses age-specific TEE prediction equations developed from the global Doubly Labeled Water (DLW) database, covering the general population including overweight/obese individuals.

🎯 Safe Weight Goals

For healthy results, aim for 0.5-1 lb/week loss or 0.5 lb/week gain. Avoid extreme diets.

📌 Important: This EER is a predicted starting point, not a definitive prescription. Self-reported dietary records typically underreport by 8–30%. Long-term body weight monitoring is the ultimate biological indicator of energy adequacy. If your weight changes unexpectedly while following this intake, adjust based on actual weight trends.
⚠️ Exclusion: These equations are not applicable to individuals with PAL > 2.5 (e.g., elite athletes, extreme manual labor) or those with specific metabolic conditions affecting energy expenditure.
